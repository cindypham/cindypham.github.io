Caravan

Less fuel, less pollution, less stress: these are some of the many benefits of carpooling. And when it comes to traveling long-distance, saving gas and money becomes exponentially more important per mile.

Caravan is a long-distance ride-sharing app concepted by YouTube intern Shane Smith. We combined our brains so that he could use his computer science skills to develop the app while I used my design savviness to create its branding identity. The result was a compelling, unified product that would appeal to college students going home during their school breaks. Road trips just got a whole lot smoother.

I compiled a branding questionnaire to help me get a better understanding of Shane's vision of his app's identity. Based on his responses, I sketched various logo ideas. I avoided using a predictable car logo that would look like any generic ride-sharing app. Instead, I leaned heavily towards incorporating wheels, since they are universal for any modes of transportation and essential tools for moving people forward.

My first round of iterations were influenced by the following qualities:

  • Minimalism. A simple, minimalist, and flat design presents a clear and straightforward experience of the app.
  • Connectivity. Dots represent the network of people that are integral to the app's function, and match the circular theme of the logo.
  • Cool colors. Caravan is unique because it provides long distance rides, so many users will likely be riding in the middle of the evening. These colors are reminiscent of night time, and blue reflects being progressive and inventive.

I narrowed down the logo decision and went through several more rounds of revising dot size and placement, as well as experimented with new color schemes. I finalized the logo to incorporate these new qualities:

  • Location marker icon. Rather than the previous wheel iterations, I chose to portray a location marker because it clearly conveyed the app's function without being too abstract.
  • Dot size variations. The dots from the initial iterations remained here because of their strong resonance for connectivity. This time, I varied the dot sizes to make the 'C' of the logo stand out more.
  • Warm color scheme. The cool colors from the initial iterations had too much of a nightclub vibe. I settled for red and orange hues reminiscent of a sunset. This still incorporated the unique experience of long-distance rides, in which users are likely to see sunsets during their long hours riding together.
