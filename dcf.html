Design Career Fair

  • Bridging young talented designers to esteemed companies

UC Davis is known and respected for many things: its high-standing veterinary program, its remarkable reputation for sustainability, and its penchant for delicious wine in its viticulture department. But what the university has yet to see is its own mark in the design world. The Design Career Fair at UC Davis, created by design students for design students, started from the ground up with the goal to strengthen and unite the creative department as well as expand career resources for students and post-grad opportunities.

With a volunteer team of 30+ designers, I was tasked with a creative director role of leading the graphics initiative and creating a cohesive visual identity for an event that would have an audience of 20+ companies and 80+ students. My team had 10 weeks to create a brand from scratch that would influence marketing campaigns, exhibits, and our website - a brand that would be reflected and used every year from its inaugural launch.

As the team lead, I directed weekly meetings, studio hours, and workshops to ensure effective brand management. To put it simply:

  • Objectives

    We laid out our goals for the annual event's branding direction to portray the following:

    • Cross-disciplinary skills. UC Davis is unique compared to art schools because of its design emphases (graphics, fashion, and interior) as well as a variety of other majors, ranging from business to agriculture.
    • A sense of community. The career fair is meant to not just bridge design students with career opportunities, but also to unite and strengthen the design community in Davis by fostering a collaborative environment.
    • The pride of self-starters. Design students recognized a need for their own career fair, and they went out and made it happen from scratch. That's something to be proud of.

    Besides creating an all-encompassing brand, we also needed to distinguish the 2016 career fair with its yearly theme: [insert future here].




  • Mood Boards

    How do you create one cohesive identity with a team of 30+ designers? We started from the basics: with post-it notes and mood boards. Gathering everyone's ideas, I led group discussions that identified common trends, with why we should implement them in our designs and why we shouldn't. Afterward, we used post-it notes to write comments and mark which designs we thought was most fitting for our objectives.

    Ultimately, the elements we finalized were:

    • Brackets. An obvious choice to reflect this year's theme, [insert future here].
    • CMYK. Four inks in color printing as a reference for every creative artist's work.
    • Big, bold, and bright. To emphasize the recognizability of the design department within UC Davis.




  • Logo Study

    The Design Career Fair logo went through many rounds of iterations. We explored different shape studies, refining it several times until we could get it just right. After weeks of blood and tears, we finalized the Tangram-style logo.

    The finalized logo encompasses several elements:

    • Tangrams. All design students at UC Davis learn about tangrams in the introductory class. By using tangrams for our logo, we are going back to our roots.
    • Origami. The logo hints at paper folding and origami to emphasize the handmade aspect of the Design Career Fair.
    • Architecture, furniture, sheets of cloth. The abstract nature of the font allows for different interpretations that reference different emphases of design in the UC Davis curriculum.
