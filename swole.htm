Swole Gear -

The sleekest innovation to hit the gym

Client

Professor John Driscoll

Problem

Swole Gear is a smart clothing concept that was conceived for a website design course at UC Davis. I was prompted to create a website, incorporating an external stylesheet with validated CSS and HTML code, about a futuristic product related to the Internet of Things. My challenge was crafting the website in less than 24 hours.

Smart clothing is a market that I wanted to tackle, particularly in application to power lifters. My experience lifting weights throughout the year made me notice that injuries to muscles and joints occur frequently to many lifters due to incorrect form. People also recorded their lifting PRs on a notepad app in their phones, an extra step that often took up important gym time if they even remembered to do so. I wanted to create a website for a product that could provide several practical uses for weight lifters while ensuring maximum efficiency for a workout.

Process

To start with the concept, I began brainstorming logistics for smart clothing that could measure activity and heart rate during exercise in a way that would prove more useful than a regular smartwatch or fitness application. I took inspiration from surfing wetsuits because of their sleek and fitted look. If the fabric was weaved with non-adhesive biosignal sensors, it could keep track of EMG, heart rate, and breathing. These sensors could send all the physiological data to the wearer's mobile device for further analysis.

Once I perfected the concept, I brainstormed how an ideal website design should look to accurately market the product. Power lifters are a niche group that have their own specific aesthetic: sleek, strong, and serious. Training for competitive weightlifting is not something to take lightheartedly. I wanted to reflect those values in my website with the color scheme and typography, as well as stay true to the smart clothing's aesthetic. Therefore, the colors were monochramtic and the text was tall and skinny to emanate the wetsuit style. Visuals also played a big part, so I wanted to incorporate graphics that showed off the product on every page, while letting it flow seamlessly through a vertical scroll.

Solution

The final website design, completed in less than 24 hours, was a vertical scroll website where each link in the navigation bar would simply scroll down to the respective section. The layout was clean and simple in order to convey information in a clear and straightforward manner. I used black, dark gray, dark red, and white in order to reflect the hardcore athletic activity that was powerlifting. John Driscoll has kept the website to show as an example for his future design students.
