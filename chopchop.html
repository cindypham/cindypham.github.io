Chop Chop

Sometimes the most fun projects are a good ol' fashioned makeover.

KC Cui, the founder of a food delivery mobile platform called Chop Chop, approached me to freshen up his app's looks. While following the app's strict brand guidelines, I went through an exhaustive process to uplift the UI in a way that creates a more confident platform and packs a bigger punch.

I immersed myself in the user flow architecture of the app. I asked myself not just what the key features of the app were, but how I could make them better. There were certain elements about the app that I thought would be compelling:

  • Map tracker. Users can watch their delivery person advancing closer to the meetup location with their food.
  • Receipt photos. Drivers can snap a picture of their receipt and have it instantly sent to the user for validation.
  • Accountability. Users who cancel their food orders after their driver has already picked up the product will be charged, and drivers will be refunded.

Phew, what an overhaul! I made it a priority to polish the UI into conveying these keywords:

  • Clarity. A minimalist design for an easier transaction.
  • Familiarity. Model the UI design and structure in a similar fashion with apps that users are likely to be familiar with already, like Uber.
  • Efficiency. The user is HANGRY. All they want is an easy-to-follow user flow and probably a giant cheeseburger.
AND FOR THE LOVE OF GOD, stop using the Chalkduster font.
