Far From Strom -

It's easy to run away, but it's harder to escape

Client

Aggie Studios

Problem

Aggie Studios is a premiere video and media production studio that produces videos for UC Davis athletics, entertainment, and campus life. While the studio was originally centered around marketing and reporting, they began stepping into a new phase to produce more creative content. For this reason, the original feature-length film, "Far From Strom", became their Special Project of the Year in 2015 and marks the studioâ€™s largest and most ambitious project to date.

The film is a coming of age story about a young, isolated girl who comes to question her place in the lifeless-suburbia she calls home. Since Aggie Studios is not prominently known for their creative work, they needed publicity to gain recognition and interest during post-production.

Process

I was heavily involved in the project during the production process. My presence at the script readings, filmings, and discussions with the director provided me an intimate knowledge of the film and its important themes. I brainstormed different concepts for a series of posters by listing down important characters and symbols in the film.

Because it was a coming-of-age story, in which the three central teenage characters were figuring out their identities, I wanted to represent them by silhouettes. The film is character-driven because it is a character study, so all posters needed to have at least one character in them. I also wanted to use a beige color palette because of the rural setting of the film. Lastly, crows and smoking cigarettes are important symbolism for escapism, so I incorporated those symbols in several posters to pay homage.

Solution

I designed a series of 6 posters with different concepts represented in each of them. These graphics were used as promotional material to advertise the film and was put up on social media across Facebook, Twitter, and Tumblr. Complete film credits were assigned at the bottom to make each poster look official and credible for Aggie Studios.
